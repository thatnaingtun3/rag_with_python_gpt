SECTION 1: ABOUT STRATEGY FIRST
================================

Overview:
Strategy First is a top private international college in Myanmar and a leading higher education institution that offers a wide range of business, management, and Information Technology IT programs in Yangon and Mandalay.

History and Establishment:
Founded: 2010 by U Aung Chit Khin
2016: Became an approved learning partner of Heriot-Watt University, offering MBA and MSc programmes

Tagline:
Ready for the future, Ready for the world

Core Description:
Strategy First was founded with the vision of becoming a world-class institution of higher education. Since establishment, it has grown into one of the most respected colleges in Myanmar, known for academic excellence, innovative programs, and commitment to student success.

Registration:
Strategy First is registered with DICA (Directorate of Investment and Company Administration)

SECTION 8: ALUMNI
==================

Strategy First International College Alumni Charter

Introduction:
The Strategy First International College was established since 2010 by U Aung Chit Khin with the aim providing business and management courses for those who were actively seeking learning opportunities to build their knowledge and education as well as to advance their careers. Initially it was started as an Institute (Strategy First Institute) and in 2024 it became an international college holding the official license issued by the Myanmar Investment Commission. There are many types of programmes and courses offered by the college. The Strategy First has been breeding many thousands of certificate holders, diploma holders and graduates every year. The number of students who studied in and graduated from the college is sizable and most of them are playing well their parts in the economy and society.

Name:
This is the community of the Strategy First International College with the successfully completed students studied in the college. The community is officially named as "SFU Alumni". The SFU Alumni is registered as a social organization.

Objectives:
1. To organize the community systematically and purposefully in accordance with the charter's principles.
2. To keep in touch with each other among members of the alumni in person as well as through communication networks.
3. To provide helps to those who are suffering from various kinds of natural disasters and tragedies.
4. To provide continuous learning opportunities to the members through various kinds of assistance.
5. To organize educational events such as conference, workshops and seminars.
6. To organize social activities such as get-together parties, trips and excursions.

Membership:
Whoever studied and completed successfully the programme can join the SFU Alumni. For this the application must be submitted to the Membership Committee by filling up the membership form together with the certificate(s) attained from the college. The membership requires the payment of member fee and it must be paid on yearly basis. A life-time membership can also be granted by the Membership Committee.

Leadership and Governing Body:
The SFU Alumni is to be governed and administered by a democratically elected group of members, namely Executive Committee (EC) led by an elected president. 

Election Process:
1. The College's president will form an Election Committee with suitable representatives of old students and staff. This committee is responsible for free and fair election of representatives of old students. The committee must apply the method of secret voting in electing the representatives or Executive Committee (EC) member and later the Central Executive Committee (CEC).
2. The constituencies are set as follows:
   - Programme (eg. ICM), subject (eg Marketing), Batch (e.g. 15) - 2 class representatives
   - The online voting system will be applied in electing the class representatives.
3. Those who are able to work in the CEC must submit their name to the Election Committee with their proposal of Alumni Activities. The online voting system will be applied in electing the CEC. The required number CEC can be fixed by the election committee.
4. Out of the elected CEC members, one President, one General Secretary, one Treasurer and one Auditor must be appointed through the process of nomination and secret voting. Before the voting, candidates must be given equal opportunities to present their plans for the alumni.
5. The elected president can form the appropriated sub working committees on the volunteer basis.
6. The Alumni can be formed in one community or different communities based on geographical locations, eg Yangon, Mandalay, etc.

Role and Responsibilities:
The President must play the leadership and executive role to undertaking various functions and activities in line with the objectives of the SFU Alumni. The tenure is two years and the CEC members are allowed to reelect in the next round unless there is no objection by election committee. The alumni is financially and administratively independent from the Strategy First College and none others. The conduct of the leadership and activities must be purely genuine being free from personal interest. It must be purely operated by the social and volunteer-oriented spirit. Hence, there will be no pay or salaries. However, for the successful and effective running of the Alumni operations and activities, necessary staff can be hired. It is necessary also to report the activities and financial status of the Alumni to all members and to call regular meeting and assembly where the members are accessible the information and raise questions concerning the published information. The Alumni is a registered social organization and it is necessary to abide the rules and regulations concerning with social organisations.
